Introducing NFluent.

Need reasons to use NFluent? Check this out!

NFluent speeds up and eases the way you write your assertions

(that we call 'checks' BTW, to avoid conflicts with all your favorite test runners)

Check.That() is all you've got to remember! before you get carried away by the super-duper-happy-dot experience of NFluent (and IntelliSense!)

NFluent accelerates your test troubleshooting sessions

With truly-helpful error messages provided, NFluent saves you a few sessions with the debugger.

Another thing that NFluent makes, in order to increase your productivity

NFluent error messages are not only useful, but reliable by nature

Indeed, the distinction between the expected value and the one to be checked is natively enforced by the NFluent syntax.

Thus, you and your mates have no more bad error message due to wrongly ordered arguments

We aim to make the most fluent .NET assertion lib ever ;-)

Indeed, there were other attempts made previously, but even the most serious alternative did not suit our needs.

See here why

NFluent transforms your tests into real documentation for your peers

Very close to plain English, the NFluent syntax makes it easier to read even for non-developers people (QA, BA)

And of course, you can keep working with your favorite test runner frameworks!

(NUnit, xUnit, Specflow, etc) Indeed, NFluent is simply an awesome assertion library, and ... she's not exclusive ;-)
