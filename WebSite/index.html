Introducing NFluent.

Need reasons to use NFluent? Check this out!

NFluent speeds up and eases the way you write your test assertions

(that we call 'checks' BTW, to avoid conflicts with all your favorite test runners)

Check.That() is all you've got to remember! before you get carried away by the super-duper-happy-dot experience of NFluent (IntelliSense-driven)

NFluent speeds up your tests troubleshooting experience

When something goes wrong within your tests, you've got less need to launch your debugger. Another thing that NFluent makes, in order to increase your productivity

NFluent ensures your tests failure messages are not only reliable, but useful too

Indeed, the distinction between the expected value and the one to be checked is natively enforced by the NFluent syntax. No need to wonder whether your mates have respected or not the assertion method arguments order, leading to possible untrusted error messages (you have better things to check in 2013!)

NFluent transforms your tests into real readable documentation for your pairs

Very close to plain English, the NFluent syntax makes it easier to read even for non-developers people (QA, BA)

And of course, NFluent lets you keep using your favorite test runner framework!

NUnit, xUnit, Specflow, etc. Whatever, NFluent is simply an awesome assertion library, and ... she's not exclusive ;-)
